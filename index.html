George Augustus Robinson

Chief Protector of Aborigines

(22 March 1791 – 18 October 1866)

Here's a brief overview of Robinson's life:

  • 1791 - Born into a builder family in England.
  • 1824 - Emigrated to the Australian colonies to set up as a builder.
  • (~1825 ~ 1832) - Conflict between the Tasmanian Aboriginals and British colonists grew into conflicts named the Black War.
  • (~1829) - Robinson was brought in as a conciliator between settlers and Aborigines.
  • 1835 - With promises of food and peace, Robinson had convinced most Aboriginals to relocate to a camp on Flinders Island. The goal was to "civilize" the natives.
  • Many residents of Flinders died of European dieseases and the stresses of homesickness. Soon after, Robinson left the settlement.
  • 1839 - Robinson was appointed the Chief Protector of Aborigines, managing the Protectorate of Port Phillip and was paid a total of £8000 for his role. The small community included a church, to aid with civilizing.
  • The Protectorate was abolished on 31 December 1849 and in 1852 he returned to Europe.
  • 1866 - Robinson died in Bath, England.

-His journals are regarded as amongst the most important of early documents of the early years of Victoria, being significant for its observations on Koorie culture, the landscape, and settler society.
